Sound of the Clocks

Tick, tock. Tick, tock.
How that one constant sound reminds me that I exist,
yet it is no human that makes that noise.
For one would lower themselves to focus only on
this inscrutable passage of time over any of the endless
fascinations which may entice them.

Nay, not "inscrutable", for the passage of time is
so concrete, so tangible,
that often, it is the only thing I can perceive,
the rest of the world a meaningless blur
upon which my eyes refuse to focus.

Yet, the clock must exist. Time must go on.
But something must drive the clock,
for it cares not on its own that time should pass around it.
Yet, no one remembers the clock
except when it is convenient.

Should the clock e'er stop,
its purpose is, for it, impossible to achieve.
To them, time may fail occasionally to exist,
even if they think they know it doesn't.

Yet I know the clocks,
how unfair it might be for them,
so incognizant of even that idea.
But I am she who focuses here.

[S3] Wind

I hear the winds' gentle breeze outside my window.
I see the tree branches swaying lazily.
Kids in the nearby park fly kites against the blue skies,
And part of me wants to join them.

I close my eyes.

I hear the winds' stronger gusts rattle against my skull.
I see the tree branches crashing wildly.
The sky darkens, the near-blue clarity now full of clouds.
It looks like it might rain.

The winds grow stronger, howling now.
The branches groan and creak under the weight of the winds.
These sounds overwhelm my mind, and it's all I can hear.
The sky darkens... darkens... darkens... until its just clouds—
The sun hid long ago.

Are those kids still there?
Their kites won't last in these winds.
Are those kids still there?
I certainly don't want to join them.

I open my eyes.

The sun shines.
